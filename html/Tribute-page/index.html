Chadwick Boseman

Chadwick Aaron Boseman was an American actor and playwright who became a highly respected movie star with several iconic roles particularly in Black Panther (2018), and for his starring roles as several pioneering Americans; Jackie Robinson in 42 (2013), James Brown in Get on Up (2014), and Thurgood Marshall in Marshall (2017).
He also had choice parts in The Express (2008), Draft Day (2014), and Message from the King (2016).

He was born on the 29th of November 1976 and raised in Anderson, S.C., by his mother, Carolyn, who worked as a nurse, and father, Leroy, who worked in a textile factory.He was born the youngest of three boys. He attended Howard University and studied acting at the Oxford Mid-Summer Program before moving to Los Angeles in 2008 to pursue his craft on the big screen.


In 2016, Boseman was diagnosed with the stage III colon cancer and he died on Friday the 28th of August 2020 as a result of complications related to colon cancer. He was 43. Although Boseman never spoke publicly about his diagnosis, according to the statement, he worked through his treatment for most of his career years. During treatment, involving multiple surgeries and chemotherapy, he continued to work and completed production for several films, including Marshall, Da 5 Bloods, Ma Rainey, and others.
